How it feels to be Hilda

It honestly feels like the only correct way to exist for me. Today I was already mentally prepared to go through another day of being addressed as [REDACTED] but then someone came to me and used my chosen name instead. It felt and still feels really good to know that I know and or have access to some actually accepting people, even if their role to me is basically that of a social worker. It is a small thing to call someone by their chosen name, but it can be the thing that makes the other persons day just like that. By saying "Hi Hilda" to me in person, you are already WAY more of and accepting and respectful person to me than some people in my family and I would very much like you more for that. The first time that someone said my name to me I was not prepared for it and was kind of schocked, but slowly having that small happiness of being seen for the first time ever was pretty overwhelming to me and I had to go to my favorite public library to calm myself down for a bit. [REDACTED] is dead, long live Hilda! I want to start being me more authentically now.

Why it feels like that?

Me being bullied and having my self-esteem be dragged down to the depths of hell and my long and painful time in the closet for all these years might have some explaining factors to all of this. I want to stop writing this now and move on to make this site pretty. See you later!

Love, Hilda (15.08.2023)
